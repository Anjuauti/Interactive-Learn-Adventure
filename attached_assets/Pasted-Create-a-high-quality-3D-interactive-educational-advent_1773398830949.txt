Create a **high-quality 3D interactive educational adventure game** called **“Spark City Adventure: From Generation to Home Electricity.”**

This game will be used in a **Science Park Exhibit** to teach children and visitors how electricity is generated, transmitted, converted, distributed, and finally used inside homes.

The experience must be a **story-driven interactive learning game** where players explore the journey of electricity from **power plant to household appliances** using **3D models, animations, puzzles, and wiring simulations**.

Target audience:
Children aged **8–16**, school groups, and families visiting a science park.

The game must be **fun, colorful, interactive, and scientifically accurate**.

---

GAME STORY

Players enter a futuristic city called **Spark City**, where the electricity system has stopped working.

A friendly AI robot guide named **Volt** helps the player restore electricity step-by-step.

Each level represents a **different stage in the journey of electricity**.

Players interact with **3D machines, electrical components, and wiring systems** to restore power.

When each level is completed, a **large colorful “Next Level” button pops up** allowing the player to continue.

---

UI DESIGN REQUIREMENTS

The interface must be designed for **kids and science exhibits**.

UI must include:

• **Bright colorful design**
• **Large readable fonts**
• **Simple icons**
• **Card-based UI panels**
• **Glowing arrows guiding interactions**

The UI should look similar to a **modern learning game**.

Instructions should appear in **large colorful cards with icons**.

3D components should look **similar to real-world machines and electrical devices**, but simplified and colorful for children.

When the game starts, show a **welcome tutorial screen**.

Display instructions like:

• Follow glowing arrows to complete missions
• Drag components to install them
• Connect wires using your mouse
• Turn switches to power the house

Each instruction appears inside **large colorful cards**.

A **Next button** starts the game.

LEVEL 1 — HYDROELECTRIC POWER GENERATION

Show a **3D hydroelectric dam model**.

3D components:

• Water reservoir
• Dam gate
• Flowing water
• Turbine blades
• Generator rotor and stator
• Electromagnetic coils

Instruction popup:

“Tap the golden gate to open the dam.”

When the gate opens:

Water flows → spins turbine → turbine rotates generator → generator produces electricity.

Scientific explanation includes:

• kinetic energy of water
• turbine mechanical rotation
• electromagnetic induction
• how generators produce **AC electricity**

Show animated **magnetic field and AC waveform**.

Mini challenge:

Players adjust water flow to maintain correct **turbine RPM**.

Correct action:

Generator lights up and electricity is produced.

Wrong action:

Show warnings like:

• turbine overload
• insufficient water flow.

After electricity generation succeeds, a **Next Level button appears**.

Components:

Rotating magnetic field → current induced in stator coils.

LEVEL 5 — ELECTRICITY ENTERS THE HOUSE

Show a **3D house model** with a **service pole and incoming power line**.

Electricity flows into the house through:

Power line → service wire → **electric meter** → **main switch** → **MCB panel** → home wiring.

Show a **3D model of the electric meter**.

Explain:

• how the meter measures electricity consumption.

Then show a **3D MCB distribution board mounted on the wall inside the house**.

Explain:

• what MCB is
• how it protects against overload and short circuit.

When electricity reaches the MCB, show **MCB switch turning ON**.
